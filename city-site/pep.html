The Pep Era

Josep Guardiola joined Manchester City on 1 July 2016. What followed is the most dominant period of management in English football history — 19 major trophies including six Premier League titles, a historic Treble, and a record-breaking 100-point season.

Josep "Pep" Guardiola is widely considered the greatest football manager of his generation, having won titles with Barcelona, Bayern Munich, and then — most prolifically — Manchester City. His tiki-taka principles evolved into something even more dynamic at City: high-tempo positional play with tactical flexibility that left opponents perpetually unsettled. His nine seasons at the Etihad have redefined what English football can look like.

Guardiola's first season at City was a sobering lesson in the demands of English football. He arrived with a squad in need of significant overhaul — particularly in defence and fullback — and despite moments of brilliance, City finished a trophyless third. The campaign exposed vulnerabilities at the back, particularly with an ageing goalkeeper situation, and Guardiola immediately identified the need for a total defensive rebuild. Still, flashes of the Pep philosophy were unmistakable, and the foundations for what came next were being laid.

The Centurions — A Record for the Ages
Premier League Champions · 100 Points

The 2017–18 season announced Pep Guardiola's Manchester City to the world with unprecedented force. Backed by a summer of surgical investment — Ederson, Kyle Walker, Benjamin Mendy, Danilo — City played football that simply had no precedent in English football. They won the Premier League with a record 100 points, 106 goals, and 32 wins, clinching the title in April with five games to spare. Kevin De Bruyne was at the height of his powers — an all-time great playmaking performance. The Carabao Cup was won 3–0 against Arsenal at Wembley. Only Liverpool, inspired by a relentless Jürgen Klopp, managed to derail the treble bid in the FA Cup semi-final and Champions League quarter-final.

⭐ HISTORIC ACHIEVEMENT

Manchester City won the English Domestic Treble — the first men's team to do so in the top flight era.

In the most gruelling title race in Premier League history, City and Liverpool pushed each other to extraordinary heights — City finishing with 98 points, Liverpool with 97 (the third and fourth highest totals ever recorded). Raheem Sterling exploded into world-class form, scoring 17 PL goals. The FA Cup final was a masterclass — Watford demolished 6–0 in one of the most dominant cup final performances ever. The Carabao Cup was won on penalties against Chelsea. Only Tottenham's away goals rule ended City's Champions League dreams in a dramatic quarter-final. The Domestic Treble was a feat of physical and mental excellence that will never be forgotten.

Liverpool's Year — De Bruyne Magical
2nd in Premier League · Carabao Cup winners
🥤 Carabao Cup
▼

This was Jürgen Klopp's Liverpool moment — the Reds ran away with the title by an extraordinary 18 points, City unable to match the pace of a team that had been building to this for years. Yet within City's season, Kevin De Bruyne delivered one of the greatest individual performances the Premier League has ever seen. He equalled Thierry Henry's all-time assist record of 20 in a season and won Player of the Year. The season was fractured by the COVID-19 pandemic — the top flight suspended for three months, resuming behind closed doors. City won a fourth consecutive Carabao Cup, beating Aston Villa 2–1 at Wembley in March before the shutdown. The Champions League ended in the Lisbon "mini-tournament" bubble, where City fell to Lyon in the quarter-finals.

Back on Top — First CL Final

Two signings defined this season. Rúben Dias arrived from Benfica in September and immediately transformed City's defensive fragility — his partnership with John Stones was one of the best centre-back pairings in Europe. Phil Foden, 20 years old, emerged as a generational talent. City recovered from an indifferent autumn to go on a 21-game unbeaten run that clinched the title by a comfortable margin. Ilkay Gündogan's sensational winter form (12 goals between January and March) was a key catalyst. The Champions League final in Porto — City's first ever — was agonisingly lost 1–0 to Chelsea, with Pep notably omitting Fernandinho and Rodri from the starting line-up. The Carabao Cup was won for a fifth consecutive season, beating Tottenham 1–0.

21–22
Title Retained — Rodrygo's Knife in the Heart
Premier League Champions · CL Semi-Finalists
🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 Premier League
▼

Another Premier League title, clinched in another intense duel with Liverpool that went down to the final week. City won 93 points but needed every one of them. The season's defining narrative, however, was the Champions League semi-final second leg at the Bernabéu — one of the most dramatic nights in the competition's history. City were winning 5–3 on aggregate with 90 minutes played, seemingly cruising to the final. Then Rodrygo struck twice in injury time for Real Madrid — the first a close-range header, the second 90 seconds later — to level the tie, before Benzema completed the comeback with a Panenka penalty in extra time. The devastation at the final whistle was palpable. The FA Cup final was lost to Liverpool 6–5 on penalties after a goalless 120 minutes.

⭐⭐ ALL-TIME GREAT — THE TREBLE

Manchester City became the first English club to win the continental Treble since Manchester United in 1999. This is the greatest season in Manchester City's history.

Erling Haaland arrived from Borussia Dortmund in the summer of 2022 and immediately shattered every goalscoring record in existence — 52 Premier League goals in a single season (previous record: 34), 62 in all competitions. But this treble was not just about Haaland. Rodri was the heartbeat, Bernardo Silva the engine, Phil Foden the flair, De Bruyne the architect. The Champions League final in Istanbul was the culmination of everything Pep had built: a 1–0 win over Inter Milan, Rodri's sweeping strike the difference. The FA Cup was won against old rivals Manchester United 2–1 — a moment of deep satisfaction. The Premier League was won in another gruelling campaign. City became the first English team to win the continental Treble since Manchester United in 1999.

Four in a Row — An English First
4th Consecutive Premier League Title — Historic
🏴󠁧󠁢󠁥󠁮󠁧󠁿 Premier League
▼

Manchester City made history again — becoming the first men's team in the history of English top-flight football to win four consecutive league titles. The feat was achieved on the final day of the season with a 3–1 win over West Ham, in yet another remarkable title battle that went to the wire with Arsenal. Phil Foden was a Ballon d'Or contender, delivering the best individual season of his career. Rodri won the Ballon d'Or in October 2024 — the first Spanish player to do so since Luis Suárez in 1960. The Champions League was undone once more by Real Madrid in a penalty shootout following a gripping 4–4 aggregate draw in the quarter-finals. The FA Cup final was lost 2–1 to Manchester United. The Carabao Cup saw an early exit.

A Difficult Reset — The Carabao Returns
3rd in Premier League · Carabao Cup winners
🥤 Carabao Cup
▼

The 2024–25 season was a stark reminder that even the greatest teams are not immune to adversity. Rodri — the heart of the team — suffered an ACL injury in September and missed the rest of the campaign. Kevin De Bruyne departed for Napoli in the summer. Without these two pillars, City struggled for consistency in the Premier League, finishing third with 71 points — Guardiola's worst league season in England. The Champions League saw City eliminated by Real Madrid before the Round of 16 for the first time since 2012–13 (losing 3–6 on aggregate in the knockout phase play-offs). The FA Cup final was lost 1–0 to Crystal Palace. However, the Carabao Cup provided relief — Nico O'Reilly's double at Wembley sealed a 2–0 win over Arsenal, giving Pep his 19th trophy as City manager.

Legacy

Pep's Place in History

📊 The Numbers

In nine seasons, Guardiola won 19 major trophies — more than any manager in Manchester City's history. His 100-point season in 2017–18 remains the Premier League record. The Treble of 2023 was the most coveted prize in English football history.

🎯 The Philosophy

Guardiola's City play positional football at its most extreme — high press, dominant ball retention, fullbacks inverting into midfield, and a relentless demand for intelligent movement. He remade English football in his image.

🌍 Global Impact

Under Guardiola, City became the most watched club side on earth. The Abu Dhabi era, accelerated by Pep's coaching genius, transformed a mid-table English club into a global sporting institution.
